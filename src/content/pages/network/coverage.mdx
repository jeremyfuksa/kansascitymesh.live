## Router approach

We only designate ROUTER on installs that meet these criteria:

1. Significant elevation (natural bluff, rooftop, or tower).
2. 24/7 power plus some form of backup.
3. Weatherproof antennas and enclosures.
4. Someone committed to maintaining it when storms or firmware updates happen.

Everyone else should stay Client or Client Base so the public channel doesnâ€™t drown in redundant rebroadcasts.

## Priority logic

We favor **coverage deserts** over perfect topology. Filling a county-sized gap matters more than squeezing a few extra miles out of an already busy area. Elevation still matters, but we balance it with population density and the ability to bridge existing clusters.

## Priority zones

### 1. West side backbone (Johnson County)

Silence from Lenexa to Olathe is the biggest hole on the map. Even indoor Heltecs pointed east would help, but we are especially interested in water towers, library rooftops, or office parks near 69 Hwy and I-435.

### 3. South metro coverage

Belton, Raymore, and Harrisonville have almost no nodes, yet their ridgelines are perfect for long-haul clients. We are looking for HOA-friendly installsâ€”attic antennas, barn roofs, church steeples.

### 4. East corridor fill-in

Independence and Blue Springs need density more than altitude. Plenty of clients exist, but they drop offline as people experiment. We need a few semi-permanent base stations pointed west toward downtown and east toward Grain Valley.

## How to evaluate placements

1. **Elevation:** Are you above nearby rooftops or treelines?
2. **Line-of-sight:** Can you draw a straight line to another cluster on the live map?
3. **Noise:** Avoid sites crowded with competing RF gear unless you have to.
4. **Sustainability:** Weatherproofed, fused, and logged so others know what itâ€™s doing.

## DIY coverage mapping

1. Run a client or tracker in your car and log RSSI/SNR.
2. Mark dead spots and post the GPX or screenshots.
3. Repeat after moving your node or upgrading antennas to quantify the improvement.
