# Code of conduct

Short version: be kind, assume good faith, no harassment.

## What we expect

- **Be welcoming.** Most of the people contributing here are figuring this out as they go. So are we. Treat questions as opportunities to help, not as a tax on your time.
- **Be specific.** Vague feedback ("this is bad") doesn't help. Specific feedback ("this paragraph buries the lede; the price should be in the first sentence") does.
- **Be honest about what you don't know.** If you've never deployed a node and you're proposing a deployment guide rewrite, say so. We can pair you with someone who has.
- **Be patient.** This is a community project. The maintainers have day jobs.

## What we won't tolerate

- Harassment, slurs, dog-whistles, or personal attacks — in PR threads, issue comments, Discord, or anywhere else this project happens.
- Doxing or sharing someone else's private info without consent.
- Political pressure campaigns or "raids" — if you have a beef with a contributor, take it up with them or with us directly. Don't mobilize.
- Bad-faith engagement: ragebaiting, sealioning, inventing controversies, demanding emotional labor from maintainers.

If you're not sure whether something crosses a line, ask. We'd rather have the conversation than assume the worst.

## Scope

This applies anywhere this project shows up: the GitHub repo (issues, PRs, discussions), the [KC Mesh Discord](https://discord.gg/yr2QTFSvzN), in-person meetups, and any public event where you're representing KC Mesh.

## Reporting

Email **hello@orangefla.me** — that's Jeremy, who currently maintains the project. Mention what happened, who was involved, and what (if anything) you'd like done about it. We'll take it seriously and we won't share your report beyond the people who need to know.

If the issue is with Jeremy specifically, escalate via the KC Mesh Discord moderators — there are several listed in the server.

## Enforcement

Depends on what happened. Range of responses:

- **A private note** — "hey, that comment came across worse than you probably meant; please rephrase." Almost always the first step.
- **A public correction** — when something needs to be addressed where it happened.
- **A temporary timeout** — removed from Discord, PR commenting disabled, for a defined window.
- **A permanent ban** — for the worst cases or for repeat behavior after timeouts.

Maintainers make these calls. We try to be proportionate and we try to err on the side of de-escalation, but we will protect contributors from people who are making this a worse place to participate.
